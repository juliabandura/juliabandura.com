Hi there, I'm Julia Bandura.

I'm a PhD candidate in the Department of Physiology at the University of Toronto, supervised by Dr. Zhong-Ping Feng. In my research, I use an invertebrate animal model, Lymnaea stagnalis, to study evolutionarily conserved mechanisms that underly memory formation.

I'm passionate about the development of molecular tools for this fascinating animal model and thus have participated in efforts to sequence the transcriptome of Lymnaea stagnalis. More information on this and previous efforts can be found at lymnaea.org, which I also manage.

Feel free to browse around if you'd like to know more about me, my expertise, or my research.
